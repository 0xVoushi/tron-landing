# Content Voice Guide

## Voice Characteristics

### We Sound Like
- A senior engineer explaining something clearly at a whiteboard
- Confident but not arrogant — we let work speak for itself
- Precise — specific numbers, technologies, outcomes
- Direct — short sentences, active voice, no filler

### We Don't Sound Like
- A marketing agency (no buzzwords, no hype)
- A corporate enterprise (no "synergize", "leverage", "solutions")
- A blog post (no "In today's fast-paced digital world...")
- A sales pitch (no "Act now!", "Limited time!")

## Writing Rules

### Do
- Use active voice: "We build fast web apps" not "Fast web apps are built by us"
- Be specific: "Shipped 40+ products in 3 years" not "Extensive experience"
- Lead with outcomes: "Your site loads in under 2 seconds" not "We optimize performance"
- Use technical terms when the audience expects them: "SSG", "TypeScript", "smart contracts"
- Keep paragraphs under 3 sentences on landing pages
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items

### Don't
- Use superlatives without proof: "best", "world-class", "industry-leading"
- Use vague language: "cutting-edge solutions", "innovative approaches"
- Use passive voice in CTAs: "A consultation can be booked" → "Book a consultation"
- Pad with filler: "It goes without saying that...", "In order to..."
- Use exclamation marks in body copy (reserve for rare emphasis)
- Write sentences longer than 25 words on landing pages

## Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms
| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| Solutions | Products, tools, systems |
| Leverage | Use, apply |
| Utilize | Use |
| Innovative | Specific: "AI-powered", "real-time", "automated" |
| Cutting-edge | Name the technology: "Next.js 15", "GPT-4o", "Solidity" |
| Empower | Enable, help, give you |
| Seamless | Fast, smooth, works without [specific friction] |
| Robust | Reliable, tested, production-grade |
| Scalable | Handles X users, grows with you |
| Synergy | Don't. Just don't. |

### Technical Terms — When to Use
- **With dev audience**: Use freely (TypeScript, SSG, WebSocket, smart contract)
- **With business audience**: Use sparingly, always explain impact ("Server-side rendering — your site shows up in Google faster")
- **In headings**: Prefer plain language, save technical depth for body copy

## CTA Language

### Primary CTAs
- "Start Your Project"
- "Book a Discovery Call"
- "Get a Free Consultation"
- "Tell Us What You're Building"

### Secondary CTAs
- "See Our Work"
- "View Case Studies"
- "Learn More"
- "How We Work"

### Avoid
- "Submit" (generic)
- "Click Here" (inaccessible, vague)
- "Contact Us" (passive — what happens after?)
- "Buy Now" (we're a studio, not a store)

## Tone by Section

| Section | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | Confident, clear, aspirational | "We build products that perform." |
| Services | Informative, specific | "Fullstack development with Next.js, React, Node, and PostgreSQL." |
| Process | Reassuring, structured | "Week 1: Discovery. Week 2: Architecture. Week 3: First deploy." |
| Testimonials | Authentic, let the client speak | Direct quotes, specific results |
| FAQ | Helpful, straightforward | Plain answers, no sales language |
| CTA | Warm, action-oriented | "Ready to build something? Let's talk." |
